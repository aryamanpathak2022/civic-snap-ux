## Pre-Interview

**Facilitator:** Thank you for participating. Today we're testing a prototype for an app called Civic Snap — it's designed to help citizens report civic issues like potholes, broken streetlights, and garbage problems. This is a paper prototype, so I'll be showing you printed screens and swapping them based on what you do. There are no wrong answers — we're testing the design, not you. Please think out loud as you go. I'll be recording this session. Is that okay?

**P1:** Sure, that's fine.

**Facilitator:** Great. Before we start, a few quick questions. What's your age range?

**P1:** 25 to 34.

**Facilitator:** How would you rate your comfort with smartphone apps, 1 to 5?

**P1:** Definitely a 5. I use a lot of apps daily.

**Facilitator:** Have you ever reported a civic issue to authorities?

**P1:** Yes. Once I called the BBMP helpline about a pothole near my apartment. Nothing happened for three weeks. I also tweeted at the municipal account once about garbage not being collected. That actually worked faster, weirdly.

**Facilitator:** Have you used any civic or government apps before?

**P1:** I tried the BBMP app once but it was really slow and confusing. I gave up after two minutes.

**Facilitator:** What frustrates you most about reporting civic issues currently?

**P1:** You never know what happens after you report. Did someone receive it? Is anyone working on it? There's zero transparency. And having to call or email feels like shouting into a void.

**Facilitator:** What would make you more likely to report an issue if you had an app?

**P1:** If I could track it like a delivery — see exactly where it is in the process — I'd definitely use it. Also, if it was quick. I'm not writing a paragraph about a pothole.

**Facilitator:** Perfect. Let's begin the tasks.

## Task 1: Account Creation & Login

**Facilitator:** Imagine you just downloaded the Civic Snap app. You want to create an account using your phone number so you can start reporting issues. Go ahead and tell me what you'd do.

*[Facilitator places Landing Page screen in front of P1]*

**P1:** Okay, so I see a screen that says "Civic Snap" with a tagline. There's a big button that says "Get Started." I'd tap that.

*[Facilitator swaps to Citizen Login screen]*

**P1:** Now it's asking for my phone number. There's a field and a "Continue" button. I'd enter my number and tap Continue.

**Facilitator:** What are you thinking at this point?

**P1:** It's straightforward. I like that it's just phone number — no email, no password. Feels quick. I wonder if it works with any Indian number though.

*[Facilitator swaps to OTP Verification screen]*

**P1:** Now it's showing an OTP screen. Six boxes for the code. There's a "Resend OTP" link and a timer saying 30 seconds. I'd enter the code I received.

**Facilitator:** What do you notice about this screen?

**P1:** The six boxes are clear. I like the timer — it tells me when to expect a resend. There's also a "Change phone number" link at the bottom, which is good in case I typed it wrong.

*[Facilitator swaps to Profile Setup screen]*

**P1:** Now it wants my name, area, and maybe a profile photo. There's a "Save & Continue" button. I'd fill in my name, select my area from a dropdown, and save.

**Facilitator:** Anything confusing here?

**P1:** The area selection — is it a dropdown or do I type it? On paper it's not clear. In a real app, I'd expect autocomplete. Also, is the photo mandatory? It doesn't say "optional."

**Facilitator:** Good observation. I'll note that.

*[Facilitator swaps to Home Dashboard]*

**P1:** Now I'm on the home screen. I see my name at the top, some quick action cards, and a section for active reports. Looks clean.

**Facilitator:** How did you feel about the onboarding flow overall?

**P1:** It was smooth. Four screens total — landing, login, OTP, profile. That feels reasonable. Not too long.

**Observations:**
- No hesitation at any step
- Understood OTP flow immediately
- Asked about area input method (dropdown vs type)
- Questioned whether photo is mandatory
- Positive overall impression

## Task 2: Report a Pothole

**Facilitator:** You're walking home and notice a large pothole on your street. You want to report it through the app. Show me what you'd do.

**P1:** I'm on the home screen. I see a big button that says "Report Issue" — that's pretty obvious. I'd tap that.

*[Facilitator swaps to Report Hazard screen]*

**P1:** Now I see a screen where I can select the type of issue. There are categories like "Road Damage," "Streetlight," "Garbage," "Water Issue." I'd tap "Road Damage."

**Facilitator:** What are you thinking?

**P1:** The categories are clear with icons. I like that. There's also a search bar — so if I can't find my category, I can search. Good.

**P1:** Now it's asking me to take or upload a photo. There's a camera icon and a gallery icon. I'd take a photo of the pothole.

**Facilitator:** What do you notice about this step?

**P1:** It says "AI auto-tags" below the photo area. So the app will automatically figure out it's a pothole? That's cool but also... I want to make sure it's correct. Can I edit the tags?

**Facilitator:** What would you expect?

**P1:** I'd want to see what tags it detected and be able to remove or add ones. Like if it says "pothole" but I also want to add "monsoon damage" or something.

*[Facilitator swaps to show the auto-detected tags section]*

**P1:** Okay, so it shows "Detected Issues: Pothole, Road Crack, Surface Damage" as little chips. I can tap to remove them. And there's a text area for additional details. I'd write "Large pothole near the bus stop, about 2 feet wide."

**Facilitator:** What about location?

**P1:** I see a "Use Current Location" button. That's convenient. There's also a pin I can drag on a map. I'd just use current location since I'm standing right there.

**Facilitator:** And then?

**P1:** There's a big "Submit Report" button at the bottom. I'd tap that. Oh, there's also a note saying "Anonymous reporting available." That's interesting — I didn't notice it before. It's a small toggle.

**Facilitator:** Would you use anonymous reporting?

**P1:** Maybe not for this. If I'm reporting a pothole, I don't mind my name being attached. But for something sensitive, like illegal construction, I'd definitely use it.

*[Facilitator swaps to Submission Confirmation screen]*

**P1:** Now it says "Report Submitted!" with a report ID — CS-2026-0847. It shows the status as "Received" and says "Estimated response: 48 hours." There's also a "Track This Report" button and a "Back to Home" button.

**Facilitator:** How do you feel about this flow?

**P1:** Very satisfying. I like the report ID — it feels official. The estimated response time sets expectations. And I can track it immediately. The only thing is, the flow felt a bit long. Photo, tags, description, location, submit — that's five steps. But each step is simple, so it's okay.

**Observations:**
- Found "Report Issue" button immediately
- Understood category selection
- Interested in AI auto-tagging but wanted editability
- Location step was clear
- Noticed anonymous reporting toggle (small, almost missed)
- Positive about report ID and estimated response time
- Felt flow was slightly long but acceptable

## Task 3: Check Report Status

**Facilitator:** You reported a broken streetlight last week. You want to check if it's been fixed. What would you do?

**P1:** I'm on the home screen. I see "Active Reports" section with two cards. One says "Streetlight — Sector 12" with status "In Progress." I'd tap on that.

**Facilitator:** Alternatively, what if you had many reports and didn't see it on the home screen?

**P1:** I'd look for a "My Reports" option. I see it in the bottom navigation bar. I'd tap that.

*[Facilitator swaps to My Reports screen]*

**P1:** Now I see a full list of my reports. Each card shows the issue type, location, date submitted, and a colored status badge. Green for resolved, yellow for in progress, red for pending. That's really clear.

**Facilitator:** Tap on the streetlight one.

*[Facilitator swaps to Live Tracking screen]*

**P1:** This is the tracking screen. I see a progress bar with stages: Received → Assigned → In Progress → Resolved → Verified. The current stage is "In Progress" highlighted. Below that, there's a delay notice saying "Awaiting materials for electrical repair. ETA shifts to March 20." 

**Facilitator:** What do you think of this screen?

**P1:** This is exactly what I wanted — transparency. I can see exactly where my report is. The delay notice is honest and helpful. There's also an "Activity Log" I can tap to see more details. And below, I see "Agency Tasks" showing what the field worker is doing. That's really cool — I can see the digital handshake steps.

**P1:** One thing though — the screen has a lot of information. The activity log and agency tasks sections are below the fold. I might not scroll down if I just want a quick status check.

**Facilitator:** Good point. Is the main status clear at a glance?

**P1:** Yes, the progress bar at the top is very clear. That's the first thing I see.

**Observations:**
- Found My Reports via bottom nav easily
- Status color coding was immediately understood
- Progress bar was the standout feature
- Delay notice appreciated for transparency
- Screen felt information-dense — might benefit from progressive disclosure
- Activity log and agency tasks are below the fold

## Task 4: Verify a Fix

**Facilitator:** You receive a notification that the pothole you reported has been repaired. The app asks you to verify the fix. What do you do?

**P1:** I'd tap the notification. 

*[Facilitator swaps to Verify Fix screen]*

**P1:** Okay, now I'm on a verification screen. I see two photos side by side — "Before" and "After." The before photo shows the pothole, and the after photo shows it filled. Below the photos, there's GPS info and a timestamp.

**Facilitator:** What would you do next?

**P1:** I'd compare the photos. If the after photo looks good, I'd tap "Confirm Fix." If not, I'd tap "Dispute." There are two big buttons at the bottom.

**Facilitator:** What do you think about the dispute option?

**P1:** There's a note saying "Disputing will trigger supervisor review within 24 hours." That's good — it tells me what happens. I feel like I need to be careful though. I wouldn't want to dispute accidentally.

**Facilitator:** Is the distinction between Confirm and Dispute clear?

**P1:** Yes. Confirm is green, Dispute is orange. The colors help. But I'd want a confirmation dialog before disputing — like "Are you sure you want to dispute this repair?"

*[Facilitator swaps to confirmation after tapping Confirm]*

**P1:** Now it says "Karma badge earned · +18 trust points added to your profile." That's a nice gamification touch. It makes me feel like my participation matters.

**Facilitator:** Would the karma system motivate you?

**P1:** Honestly, yes. It sounds silly but getting points and badges would make me want to report more issues and verify more fixes. It turns civic duty into something rewarding.

**Observations:**
- Before/after comparison was clear and intuitive
- GPS + timestamp added trust
- Wanted confirmation dialog before disputing
- Karma/trust points were well-received
- Color coding (green/orange) for actions was effective
- Gamification seen as motivating, not childish
