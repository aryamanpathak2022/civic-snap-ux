## Pre-Interview

**Facilitator:** Thank you for participating. We're testing a digital prototype for Civic Snap — an app for reporting civic issues. Think out loud. I'll be recording. Is that okay?

**P4:** Yes, that's fine.

**Facilitator:** Age range?

**P4:** 45 to 54.

**Facilitator:** Comfort with apps, 1 to 5?

**P4:** I'd say 3. I use WhatsApp, YouTube, and my banking app. But I'm not someone who explores new apps often.

**Facilitator:** Ever reported a civic issue?

**P4:** Yes, many times. I've called the municipal office about water supply issues, garbage collection problems, and road damage. Sometimes it works, sometimes it doesn't. The most effective method has been visiting the ward office in person, which is inconvenient.

**Facilitator:** Used any civic apps?

**P4:** My son showed me the Swachh Bharat app once. It was confusing — too many options and the text was small. I gave up.

**Facilitator:** Biggest frustration?

**P4:** You report something and then nothing happens. Or if it does happen, you never know who to thank or complain to. There's no accountability. Also, many apps have text that's too small for older eyes.

**Facilitator:** What would make you use an app?

**P4:** If it's simple — big buttons, clear text, not too many steps. And if I can see that my report actually leads to action. I don't want to waste time on something that doesn't work.

**Facilitator:** Let's begin.

## Task 1: End-to-End Report Flow

*[Facilitator opens Landing Page]*

**P4:** The screen is dark. I can see "Civic Snap" in white text. The "Get Started" button is blue and big — easy to see. I'd tap that.

*[Taps → Phone Login]*

**P4:** Now it wants my phone number. The +91 is already there. The text is a good size — I can read it without squinting. I'd enter my number and tap Continue.

**Facilitator:** How's the text size?

**P4:** Good. Much better than most apps. I don't need my reading glasses for this.

*[Taps → OTP]*

**P4:** Six boxes for the code. The numbers are large and clear. I can see the timer and the resend option. The "Change phone number" link is at the bottom — easy to find if I made a mistake.

*[Enters OTP → Profile Setup]*

**P4:** Profile setup. I see "Add Profile Photo" with "Optional" written clearly. I'd skip the photo. The name field is straightforward. The area is already filled in as "Ward 86 · Indiranagar" — it says it's auto-detected. That's convenient. I like that it explains why they need my area.

*[Taps Save & Continue → Home Dashboard]*

**P4:** Home screen. I see "Good morning, Priya Sharma" at the top. There's a star icon with 142 next to it — I'm not sure what that means yet. And a bell icon with a red dot — probably notifications.

**P4:** Below that, three big buttons: "Report Issue" in blue, "My Reports" and "Map View" in white. The blue one stands out — that's clearly the main action.

**Facilitator:** What do you think about the star with 142?

**P4:** I'm curious but I'd explore it later. First I'd try reporting something.

**P4:** I see "Active Reports" with two cards. One has a yellow badge saying "In Progress" and a little bar showing progress. The other is green saying "Resolved." The colors make it easy to understand — yellow means still happening, green means done.

**P4:** Below that, "Nearby Issues" with a small map. I can see colored dots on the map. I'd tap "Report Issue."

*[Taps Report Issue → Category]*

**P4:** Now I see categories with pictures. Road Damage has a road icon, Streetlight has a sun icon, Garbage has a bin icon. The pictures help — I don't have to read every word. I'd tap "Water Issue" for the broken pipe.

**Facilitator:** How does this compare to the paper version you saw?

**P4:** The paper version had text labels. These pictures make it much easier. I can find what I need without reading carefully.

*[Taps Water Issue → Photo & Tags]*

**P4:** Now it wants a photo. There are two buttons — Camera and Gallery. I'd tap Camera to take a new photo. Below that, it shows some tags that the app detected automatically. I can remove ones that are wrong with the X button. And there's an "Add Tag" button with a plus sign — I can add my own tags too.

**P4:** There's a space to write details, and a map showing my location with a pin I can drag. The "Use Current Location" button is blue and easy to find.

*[Taps Continue → Review & Submit]*

**P4:** This screen shows a summary of everything I've entered. I can review it before submitting. There's a green box saying it's been routed to "BBMP Roads Department" with an estimated response of 48 hours. That's good — I know who's handling it and when to expect a response.

**P4:** And there's an "Anonymous Reporting" option with a toggle switch. It says "Your name won't be visible to agencies." That's clear. I'd leave it off for a water issue.

*[Taps Submit → Confirmation]*

**P4:** Big green checkmark. "Report Submitted!" with a report number — CS-2026-0847. It shows the status, category, department, and estimated response time. I can track the report or go back home.

**Facilitator:** How was the overall experience?

**P4:** Very smooth. The text is readable, the buttons are big enough, and each step is clear. I didn't feel lost at any point. The whole thing took maybe two minutes.

**Observations:**
- Praised text size and readability (important for older users)
- Found category icons helpful — "don't have to read every word"
- Understood color coding immediately
- Anonymous toggle was clear and well-placed
- Confirmation screen provided all needed information
- Estimated total time: ~2 minutes
- No confusion at any step

## Task 2: Track & Verify a Report

**P4:** I'd tap "Reports" at the bottom of the screen.

*[Taps Reports → My Reports]*

**P4:** I see my reports. The yellow one says "In Progress" and the green one says "Resolved." I'd tap the yellow one.

*[Taps → Tracking Simple View]*

**P4:** This screen shows the progress of my report. I can see five steps: Received, Assigned, In Progress, Resolved, Verified. The first two have green checkmarks, the third one is blue and active, and the last two are grey. It's like a checklist — I can see exactly where things are.

**P4:** There's a yellow box with a warning icon saying there's a delay — "Awaiting electrical parts. New ETA: March 20." That's honest. I appreciate that they tell me instead of just going silent.

**P4:** Below that, there's a "Show Details" button. I'd tap it to see more.

*[Taps Show Details → Detailed View]*

**P4:** Now I see more information. "Activity History" shows what's happened — when work started, when the delay was noticed, when it was assigned. Each with a date and time. And "Field Worker Tasks" shows a checklist of what the worker needs to do. Two are done (green), one is in progress (blue), and two are pending (grey).

**Facilitator:** Is this too much information?

**P4:** No, because I chose to see it by tapping "Show Details." If I just wanted a quick check, I could stay on the simple view. Having both options is good — some people want details, some don't.

**P4:** I'd tap "Hide Details" to go back. The simple view is what I'd use most often.

**Observations:**
- Progress steps immediately understood as a "checklist"
- Delay notice appreciated for honesty
- Chose to explore details voluntarily
- Understood the value of simple vs detailed views
- No terminology confusion ("Progress Steps" and "Field Worker Tasks" were clear)
